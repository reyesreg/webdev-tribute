  2. Carlo Estrada
    Interviewee for WEBDEVT
    De La Salle-College of St Benilde
    Who is your Hero?
    My heroes are my mom and
    my dad.

    Asking Carlo about who he considers to be his hero proved to be difficult, as he couldn't really choose only one.

    "My heroes are my mom and my dad," he proudly says.

    From an early age, Carlo recognized the hard work and effort his parents had to go through for the sake of the family. Even in the face of adversary and hardships, they always remained a strong foundation for support and love. Because of the sacrifices they have made, Carlo always strives to make his parents proud through his studies, his career, and his music.

  3. A Bit About the Family

    Carlo is the second child among three siblings, all of whom are currently in University in their corresponding courses. The eldest, a sister, is graduating in a few months and aspires to become a nurse. The youngest, also a sister, is currently studying in Universiry of Santo Tomas. And of course, Carlo is studying in DLS-CSB, taking up Information Systems.

    They're hard-working.

    Hearing about him and his siblings all being in University at the same time, I couldn't help but feel at awe how his parents manage their financials, especially when tuition fees nowadays are at an all-time high. I commented on how difficult it must be for his parents to support all of them at the same time. "They're hard-working," he replied with a smile.


  4. Joanne Estrada Not just another mom

    Not everything in this life comes cheap or easy, and Carlo's mom is a living testament to that fact. "She left when I was younger and I haven't seen her in about seven years."

    Joanne Estrada is an Overseas Filipino Worker. She 49 years old, and has been a medical assistant in the States for over seven years. She regularly sends money to her family for support. Although they haven't physically seen each other in a very long time, Carlo and his mom regularly speak over Viber.

    "I miss her very much," he tells me.

    I haven't seen her in seven years.

  5. Danny Estrada Not just another dad

    Between the two parents, it makes perfect sense why Carlo considers himself to be closer to his dad more than his mom. "He's always been there for me, physically and emotionally, but that doesn't make my mom any less of a hero to me."

    Danny Estrada, or Carlos dad, earns a living through dentistry. He is 59 years old and very much going strong. "He works very hard", Carlo says, "Even if he's very tired, he will never miss a single day of work."

    He's always been there for me, physically and emotionally

  6. Danny Estrada Not just another dad

    I asked Carlo if he had any special experiences with his father that he'd like to share. "I remember in High School, we had a father-son activity in one of our recollections," Carlo fondly remembers, "me and my dad had a one-on-one talk. I don't wanna get into details, but he apologized for his wrongdoings, and so did I."

    Reflecting on this story, I can't help but compare my High School recollection when we had to do the same one-on-one talk with our parents. Carlo's right, it was one of the best things I've experienced with my parents as well.
